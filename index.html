Garlic Butter Pasta

There's something incredibly comforting about a bowl of garlic butter pasta that makes it my go-to dish on busy weeknights. The aroma of garlic sizzling in butter is enough to make anyone's mouth water, and the best part? It's ready in the time it takes to boil the pasta.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz of dried spaghetti (I prefer the bronze-cut variety for its rough texture that holds onto the sauce)
  • 4 tbsp of unsalted butter (rich and creamy, it's the base of our sauce)
  • 4 cloves of garlic (freshly minced for that punchy flavor)
  • 1/4 cup of freshly grated Parmesan cheese (the real deal, not the pre-shredded stuff)
  • 1/4 tsp of crushed red pepper flakes (for a subtle heat that lingers)
  • Salt (to season the pasta water)
  • Freshly chopped parsley (for a bright, herby finish)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil over high heat. The water should taste like the sea to properly season the pasta.
  2. Add the spaghetti to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions until al dente, about 8-10 minutes. Tip: Stir the pasta occasionally to prevent sticking.
  3. While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Watch for the butter to foam slightly, which means it's ready for the garlic.
  4. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes to the skillet, sautéing for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned. Tip: Garlic burns quickly, so keep the heat moderate and stir constantly.
  5. Reserve 1/2 cup of the pasta water, then drain the spaghetti and add it directly to the skillet with the garlic butter.
  6. Toss the pasta in the garlic butter sauce, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water to help the sauce cling to the noodles. Tip: The starchy pasta water is key to creating a silky, emulsified sauce.
  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until melted and creamy.
  8. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness.
